// How Texas hail deductibles actually work
It's not a flat fee. It's 2% of your home's value.
In Texas and most hail-prone states, your deductible is calculated as a percentage of your home's insured value — not a fixed dollar amount. On a $400,000 home, that's $8,000 out of pocket before your insurance company contributes a single dollar. On a $500,000 home, it's $10,000. And it resets on every claim.
⚠  Oklahoma, Colorado, Kansas, and Nebraska use the same model. Pull your declarations page and look for "windstorm/hail deductible."
